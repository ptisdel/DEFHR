Horses of all ages, breeds, and experience levels are available for adoption to homes in the Mid-Atlantic region. Scroll right through this panoramic view of Days End Farm and click on the buttons to learn more about the road to adoption.

Arrival. DEFHR primarily cares for horses impounded by local animal control authorities and humane agencies. When they arrive, these horses can have a range of health issues and experience—from well-trained pleasure horses to feral horses who have limited prior contact with humans.

Restoring health. Staff develop a care and nutrition plan for each horse to help them gain weight and reach good physical health, a process that can take anywhere from 4 months to a year.

Keeping records. Throughout rehabilitation, DEFHR documents each horse’s medical and dental condition, and to the extent possible, the horse’s medical history, in detail.

Customized training. Upon completing rehabilitative care, each horse is evaluated to determine their level of training experience. Following the evaluation, DEFHR develops a customized training plan for each horse.

Ready for adoption. DEFHR horses available for adoption are shown by appointment only. Before scheduling an appointment, visitors must fill out a Horse Interest Form. Upon receiving the form, a DEFHR staff member will schedule an appointment.

Applying for adoption. Applying for adoption is easy! After you are matched with the right horse, you will receive an application. Simply fill it out and return it, along with the application fee, and we will handle it from there.

Going home. Upon acceptance of your application, you must sign an adoption agreement, pay a non-refundable adoption fee, and coordinate transportation for the horse to its new home.

Following up. DEFHR retains ownership of each adopted horse for a minimum of 2 years after adoption. Pending an approved final farm inspection and satisfactory horse care provided by the adopter, they may be granted legal title at the discretion of DEFHR and its Board of Directors.

Making progress. Supported by generous professional trainers and a dedicated team of volunteers, DEFHR works to help each horse progress their skills as an equine partner, making them more suitable for adoption.

Horse and handler. Prior to placement, an adopter should expect to make two or more visits with the horse so DEFHR can determine compatibility. Each horse and handler are evaluated as a pair to ensure an appropriate match.
